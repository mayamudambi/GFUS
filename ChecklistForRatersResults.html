The Criteria for Useful Science Checklist: Results

Criteria Result Suggestions Rater Comments
1. Findings are useful 50
1a) Importance of problem explained by title, abstract, result tables, or conclusions
1b) Findings provide improved prediction, decision-making, policy, or methods 50
1c) Findings shown to be surprising to practitioners?
1d) Findings shown to be surprising to researchers? 50
1e) Directional findings shown to be surprising? 50
1f) Effect size of findings shown to be surprising? 50
2. Comprehensive review of prior scientific knowledge? 50
2a) Search procedures shown to be objective with respect to reasonable hypotheses?
2b) Search procedures shown to be comprehensive with respect to reasonable hypotheses?
2c) Contacted authors who were on cited on substantive matters to ensure that the summary of their findings was correct, the references were correct, and that no key studies were overlooked.
3. Full disclosure
3a) Multiple reasonable hypotheses: Prior and revisions (e.g., direction and magnitude of causal effects)?
3b) Conditions for hypotheses: Prior and revised?
3c) Methods are fully described and easy to understand
3d) Data are easily accessible using information provided in the paper
3e) Other information needed for understanding (e.g. reviewers if acceptable, acknowledgements, shortcomings, potential biases) provided
4. Data are valid and reliable
4a) Data were shown to be relevant to the problem
4b) All relevant data (multiple measures) were used to help ensure validity and compensate for biases
4c) Reliability of data was assessed
4d) Longest available series used when analyzing time series data
5. Methods were valid and simple
5a) Methods were shown to be relevant for the problem, unless obvious to all
5b) Multiple validated methods were used
5c) Methods used cumulative scientfic knowledge explicitly
5d) Methods were sufficiently simple for all potential users of the findings to understand
6. Experimental evidence was used to test all reasonable alternative hypotheses
6a) All reasonable hypotheses were compared using experimental evidence under explicit conditions
6b) Predictive validity of hypotheses were tested using out-of-sample data
7. Conclusions are consistent with the evidence
7a) Conclusions are logically consistent with the evidence presented in the paper
7b) Conclusions contribute to cumulative scientific knowlege on the problem addessed by the paper
